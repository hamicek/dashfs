# Reading Notes

## Key Papers

### "Intelligent Tutoring Systems: An Overview" (2023)
- Authors: Smith et al.
- Main findings:
  - ITS improve learning outcomes by 0.76 standard deviations
  - Most effective for STEM subjects
  - Adaptive feedback is key component
- Relevance: Foundation for literature review

### "Large Language Models in Education" (2024)
- Authors: Johnson & Lee
- Main findings:
  - LLMs can provide personalized explanations
  - Concerns about accuracy and hallucinations
  - Need for human oversight
- Relevance: Current state of AI tutoring

### "Ethical Considerations of AI in Classrooms" (2024)
- Authors: Garcia et al.
- Key points:
  - Privacy concerns with student data
  - Bias in AI systems
  - Digital divide issues
- Relevance: Important for discussion section
